## Research Goal

General-purpose brief for exploring the CTAHR document collection. Use this brief when the research query spans multiple topics or when no specialized brief exists yet.

## Relevance Criteria

### Tier 1 - Primary Relevance (INCLUDE)
Documents that provide substantive research content on the queried topic:
- Research papers, technical reports, or detailed guides
- Documents with methods, results, data, or actionable recommendations
- Extension publications with in-depth coverage of a specific subject

### Tier 2 - Secondary Relevance (INCLUDE WITH CAVEAT)
Documents with partial relevant content:
- Faculty bios or profiles that include research descriptions or publication lists
- Presentation slides with technical content (note: may need Read tool for full review)
- Annual reports or newsletters with sections on the topic
- Documents where the topic is secondary to the main subject

### Tier 3 - Peripheral (EXCLUDE)
Documents that match keywords but lack substantive content:
- Administrative documents, meeting agendas, or organizational charts
- Documents that mention the topic only in a list or passing reference
- Duplicate versions of the same document (keep the most complete version)
- Flyers or announcements with no technical content

## Extraction Priorities

When reading relevant documents, capture these details in order of importance:

1. **Core content** — Main research findings, methods, or recommendations
2. **Authors and affiliations** — Who produced this work and their department/program
3. **Scope and context** — What geographic area, time period, or conditions apply
4. **Data and measurements** — Specific numbers, statistics, or quantified results
5. **References and connections** — Citations to other works, related documents, or follow-up studies

## Extraction Taxonomy

| Tag | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| `research` | Original research findings, experiments, data |
| `method` | Techniques, procedures, protocols |
| `recommendation` | Practical advice, best practices, guidelines |
| `review` | Literature review, state of knowledge summary |
| `species` | Specific organisms, crops, or livestock discussed |
| `region` | Geographic locations or conditions mentioned |

## Bias Awareness

Patterns that might skew results for general collection exploration:

- **Collection coverage:** Some CTAHR programs may be better represented than others in the collection. Note which collections are producing results.
- **Document type:** PDFs with full text dominate search results. Slide decks (PPTX) and images may contain relevant visual content but have less searchable text.
- **Recency:** Older documents may have poorer text extraction. Image-only scanned PDFs won't appear in text searches.
- **Format bias:** Research papers are easier to search than extension guides, which may use more visual layouts.

## Search Strategy

### Primary Terms
Start with the researcher's topic keywords:
- Direct topic terms (e.g., "soil conservation", "pest management")
- Scientific names when applicable

### Secondary Terms
Broaden or refine:
- Synonyms and related concepts
- Abbreviations and acronyms common in CTAHR context
- Specific species, methods, or locations

### Term Variations
- Try singular and plural forms
- Try both common and scientific names
- Try acronyms (e.g., "IPM" for integrated pest management)
