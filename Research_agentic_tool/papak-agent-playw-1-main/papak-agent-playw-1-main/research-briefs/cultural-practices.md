# Research Brief: Cultural Practices

## Metadata
- **Created:** 2024-12-17
- **Query Type:** Cultural practices / traditional knowledge
- **Version:** 1

## Research Goal

Find newspaper articles that describe **how traditional Hawaiian practices were performed** - methods, techniques, regional variations, and practical knowledge. This includes agriculture (farming, fishing), crafts, ceremonies, and daily life practices.

## Relevance Criteria

### Tier 1 - Primary Relevance (INCLUDE)
- Article's primary focus is describing the practice
- Contains instructions, methods, or detailed descriptions
- Discusses regional variations or specific techniques
- Titled as instructional or descriptive of the practice
- Examples: "Na Loina Kanu Kalo" (The traditions of planting taro)

### Tier 2 - Secondary Relevance (INCLUDE WITH CAVEAT)
- Practice is discussed but not the primary focus
- Contains useful details embedded in broader content
- Compares traditional vs. modern methods
- Note the limited scope in summary
- Examples: Article about a region that includes a section on local farming methods

### Tier 3 - Peripheral (EXCLUDE)
- Only mentions the practice in passing
- Uses practice as context for unrelated content
- Exhortations to do something without explaining how
- Examples: News article saying "plant kalo" without cultivation details

## Extraction Priorities

When reading relevant articles, capture these details in order of importance:

1. **Methods/techniques** - Step-by-step or descriptive how-to information
2. **Regional specifics** - Which areas, what local variations
3. **Named varieties/types** - Specific names (only if clearly readable in OCR)
4. **Timing/seasons** - When to do things, seasonal considerations
5. **Materials/tools** - What is used in the practice
6. **Author/source credentials** - Practitioner vs. observer vs. institution
7. **Connections to other practices** - Related or prerequisite knowledge

## Bias Awareness

Patterns that might skew results for this research type:

- **Institutional bias:** Government/experiment station articles may dominate search results. These provide useful information but represent Western scientific framing. Traditional practitioner voices may be underrepresented.

- **Time period bias:** 1900s articles may be more explicitly instructional (documenting declining knowledge) while 1800s articles may assume baseline cultural knowledge and be less explicit about methods.

- **"Instructional" framing:** Articles written AS instructions are easier to find than traditional knowledge embedded in mo'olelo (stories) or casual descriptions.

- **Regional coverage:** Some regions may have more newspaper coverage than others. Absence of information doesn't mean absence of practice.

## Search Strategy

### Primary Terms
Hawaiian terms for the practice itself:
- Practice verb: kanu (plant), mahi (cultivate), lawaia (fish), etc.
- Practice noun: the thing being practiced (kalo, i'a, kapa, etc.)
- Instruction words: kuhikuhi (instructions), ao (teach/learn)

### Secondary Terms
Terms that may surface traditional knowledge:
- loina (tradition/custom)
- hana (work/practice)
- oihana (occupation/industry)
- kupuna / wa kahiko (ancestors / ancient times)

### Term Combinations
Effective patterns:
- [practice] + [region]: "kanu kalo Waipio"
- [instruction word] + [practice]: "kuhikuhi kalo"
- [tradition word] + [practice]: "loina mahi ai"
- [practice] + [type]: "kalo maloo" (dryland taro)

## Notes

### OCR Challenges for This Research Type
- Variety names and specialized vocabulary are often garbled
- When OCR prevents accurate extraction, note this explicitly
- Recommend researcher view original scan for lists of names/varieties
- Numbers and measurements usually survive OCR better than names

### Series Articles
Many instructional articles were published as series ("Aole i pau" = not finished). When finding one part, note this and consider searching for continuation.

### Balancing Sources
Actively seek both:
- Institutional sources (experiment stations, government) - often clearer methods
- Community sources (letters, regional news, mo'olelo) - often more traditional perspective

Document the mix of sources in results and note if one type dominates.
